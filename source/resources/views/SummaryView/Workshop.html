Workshop Hints  


About the Workshop Hints Pages

Computer software such as Miradi can be a great tool for helping individuals or small project teams to go step-by-step through a complex process like that presented in the CMP Open Standards. We have found, however, that software does not work very well if you are working with a group of more than two or three people. Too often, it seems that if you use software with a larger group, even if you are projecting the display in large size, most people in the room become passive observers, watching the person at the keyboard go through the steps.

To this end, we strongly recommend that if you are working with more than three people, you should NOT use Miradi as your primary work environment. Instead, you need to use basic workshop tools like flip charts and sticky tarps. You can still use Miradi software, however, as a recording device to capture the work that goes on in the workshop setting.

To this end, the context sensitive Workshop Hints pages will give you advice on how to develop and run a workshop covering each step in the CMP Open Standards.


Types of Workshops

There are three main types of workshops that people commonly hold, each with its own set of logistical challenges. All four, however, involve going through the basic step-by-step process outlined in the CMP Open Standards and in Miradi.

  1. Basic Project Workshop - Taking a project team that typically consists of 3-10 individals through the steps. In these workshops, most work will take place in plenary sessions (everyone meets together).
  2. Multi-Project Workshop - Bringing together a number of projects together (typically 2 to 8) so that they can go through the steps together. In these workshops, although instruction may happen in plenary, most work will take place in breakout groups, punctuated with report-back sessions. This approach has the advantage of creating economies of scale because one or two trainers can reach out to multiple projects. It also enables the project teams to interact with and learn from one another.
  3. Large-Project Workshop - Taking a project team that consists of a large number of stakeholders (often 25-50) through the steps. In these workshops, it is often necessary to divide the team into smaller sub-teams that each work on one or more targets and associated chains of factors.

In addition to the basic workshops outlined above, there are also several variants:

  1. Training Course - Teaching students and other people interested in learning how to apply the CMP Open Standards through a basic training course. In these courses, small groups of students will go through the steps using one or more "practice" projects.
  2. Distance Learning - Teaching practitioners and students how to apply the CMP Open Standards through a self-directed online training program.
