Coming Attractions

The Miradi software is still in its initial development phase.  We are currently working on developing many new functions that will be rolled out in the coming months.  A few highlights that we are hoping to complete are listed below.  If you have additional ideas or requests, please contact us at:  feedback@miradi.org.  And please check the Miradi website (www.Miradi.org) for the latest version of the software - we release new versions on a regular basis.

Application Wide

  • More Interactive Interview Screens - Improvements to the various interview screens that walk you through the steps in the CMP Process.
  • More Examples - The addition of more example of conservation projects from around the world.
  • Tools to Support Later Steps in the CMP Open Standards - The current version of Miradi is largely focused on the planning phase of the adaptive management cycle; future tools will help in the analysis and learning phases.
  • Version Control - Ability to save and revert to versions of your project and to share your project with team members working at remote locations.
  • Translations to Different Languages - Miradi is now available in several different languages such as Spanish and Bahasa Indonesia.  See the Miradi.org website for more details. If you are interested in helping us develop a version for your language, please contact us.
  • Adaptations to Other Disciplines - Miradi currently focuses on biodiversity conservation projects.  However, this adaptive management approach undoubtedly will be of use to other disciplines as well, such as development projects, public health projects, or many others.  If you are interested in helping us to develop a version for your field, please contact us.

Summary View

  • Customizable Tabs - The ability to create a custom tab that contains only the data fields that your project or organization wants to track.
  • Summary Dashboard - The ability to create a dashboard for your project showing the status of key fields and indicators.

Diagram View

  • Diagram Slide Show - The ability to hide or display specific combinations of factors and then "snap" images that can be used to develop an animated presentation of your model.
  • Support for Human Welfare Targets - The ability to display targets related to human (as opposed to natural world) welfare.

Threat Ratings View

  • Improved Interface for Stress-Based Threat Ratings - Improved user interface for doing TNC-style Stress and Source of Stress Ratings.

Planning View

  • Continued Improvement of User Interface - Enhancements to the presentation of information in these screens.
  • Analytical Tools - Tools to help conduct basic analyses of monitoring data.
  • Reports by Accounting Code and Donor - The ability to develop reports showing planned expenditures by donor or by accounting code.
  • Tracking of Actual Expenses - Tools to enter actual expenditures and to match these up to budget line items and report on them by programmatic objectives and activities, by accounting codes, or by funding sources, using a Quicken or Quickbooks style interface.

Reports

  • Enhanced Import and Export - Enahanced ability to export information in various formats.
  • Custom Report Generation - The ability to automatically generate documents that contain the core of material needed for reports to key conservation donors and organizations.
  • Uploads to Databases of Conservation Practice - The ability, if you so desire, to upload some or all of your project information to standard databases of conservation practice that can be accessed by practitioners around the world. 

Schedule (New View)

  • Project Calendar - A calendar showing activities/tasks and major events on a given day, week, or month either for specific team members, or for the project as a whole.
  • Gantt Chart - The ability to create a chart showing how different activities/methods and tasks link to one another and overlap among human and other resources.

Map (New View)

  • Basic Mapping - Tools to obtain a base map of your project site, outline the scope of your project area, and show targets, threats, and other objects on the map as points or polygons.
  • Basic Spatial Analyses - Functions to analyze data spatially (union or intersection of polygons, etc).
  • Analyze Spatial Variation in Key Factors - Tools to how how target or threat status varies over your project site, depict the spatial "footprint" of a given strategy or objective, and prioritize strategies and actions or monitoring work in different sections of your project area.
  • Share Data - Upload project information to spatially referenced databases.

Library (New View)

  • Store and Manage Project Documents - Store and manage images, documents, data sets, and other files related to your project.
