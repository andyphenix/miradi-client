Guide to the Stress Based Threat Rating Summary Table

The far left hand column is a listing of all threats and the top row is a listing of all targets for your project. To sort the table, click on any cell in the header row to sort by that column.You can move or change the width of columns and change the height of rows.

The cells of the table show the overall threat ratings for each threat on each target that it affects. You can click on a cell to see the details that go into its threat ratings. To create a new link between a threat and target, right (alt) click on a blank cell in the table.

Finally, the table contains three types of summary ratings:

  • The column on the far right contains a summary of the overall rating of each threat across all targets.
  • The row on the bottom contains a summary of the overall threat rating for each target.
  • Finally, the lowest right hand cell contains a summary of the threat rating for your entire project.

These summary ratings are not entered directly, but are calculated as you rate the specific effects of threats on targets. Miradi calculates threat ratings using a rule-based system. The scope and severity are used to calculate the magnitude, and the contribution and irreversibility criteria are combined to calculate a source rank. The magnitude and source ranks are then used to calculate the summary stress rating and the summary stress ratings are then used to calculate the rank of a threat to a target which is what is displayed in the threat ratings table.
