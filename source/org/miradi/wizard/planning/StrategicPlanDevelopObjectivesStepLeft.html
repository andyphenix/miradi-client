Objectives are linked to threat and opportunity factors in a model. Objectives can be either created in a conceptual model or in a results chain, although more and more people are linking them to results chains. Some people like to create them before they have identified their strategies; other people like to develop them after they have their strategies.

To create a new objective, double click on a factor and then click on the <Objectives> tab in the properties dialogue box. Click on the <Create Objective> button. Fill in the following fields:

  • Objective ID: A brief code used to identify and sort your objectives and that will appear in the diagram. One common system is to assign the letters given to the threat that the objective affects, and then a number if there is more than one objective for this threat (e.g., "FISH1" or "LOG3").

  • Objective Name: The title of the objective. Generally 3-5 words (e.g., Reduce Shark Fishing)

  • Text: The complete objective statement that meets the criteria for a good objective.
  • Comments: Any additional comments about the objective.

If you want to use an existing objective from another factor, click on the <Clone Objective> button and follow the instructions. To edit or delete an objective, click on the desired row and then either edit the text in the box at the bottom or click on the <Delete Objective> button.

You can also view and edit all your project's objectives using the "Objectives Only" setting in the <Planning View>.
