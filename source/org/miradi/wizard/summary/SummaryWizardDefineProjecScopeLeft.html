Click on the <Scope> tab and add your project's scope to the appropriate fields.

The Scope Name field should contain a 3-5 word summary title of your scope. The Scope Text field should contain the complete description of your project's scope. If you have a site-based project, you can also add the total hectares that your project covers to the Project Area field (this generally refers to the area of biodiversity of interest) and record any detail about this estimate in the Notes field.

You can also fill out the remaining fields on this page if desired, especially if this information will be helpful to have in reports generated from your Miradi file. Red List Species is used to record any endangered species found at your project site. Other Notable Species can be used to record any additional species that you want to note. Human Population refers to the number of people within the scope of the project, while the Notes field can be used to record any detail. Social Context is meant to be used for a text description of the human context at the site.
